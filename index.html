Your program at a glance

A 2-gym + 2-to-3-climb week is the sweet spot for bouldering and fat loss. The gym work is built around antagonists (push, rear delts, core), lower body strength, and finger and grip conditioning — exactly the gaps climbing alone won't fill.

Weekly template

  • Mon — Gym A (Push and Core)
  • Tue — Climb
  • Wed — Rest or GOWOD mobility
  • Thu — Gym B (Legs and Pull)
  • Fri — Rest
  • Sat — Climb
  • Sun — Optional easy climb or zone-2 cardio

Why this works for you

  • Climbing handles your pulling strength — gym fills push and leg gaps.
  • 48 hours between climb and gym means better recovery for fingers and forearms.
  • Core and anti-rotation carries over directly to harder boulders.
  • 2 strength days keeps fat-loss deficit tolerable without wrecking climbs.

Alternative splits

If you only climb twice a week

Mon Gym A, Tue Climb, Wed Rest, Thu Gym B, Fri Climb, Sat zone-2 cardio (30-45 min), Sun rest. Optionally add a 3rd short gym day (20 min conditioning + core) on Saturday.

If you climb three times a week

Mon Gym A, Tue Climb, Wed Rest, Thu Climb, Fri Gym B, Sat Climb, Sun rest. Keep intensity high only 1-2 climb days; the third should be volume or technique.

Deload every 4th week

Every fourth week, drop gym volume by ~40% (fewer sets, not lighter weight) and climb at 70% intensity. You'll come back stronger and injury-free.

Gym A — Push, Antagonists and Core

Goal: balance all the pulling you do climbing. Strong push, rear-delt, and core make you injury-proof and shoulder-stable on hard moves. ~60 min.

Warm-up (5-8 min)

  • 3 min easy row or bike
  • Band pull-aparts x 20
  • Scap push-ups x 10
  • Wall slides x 10
  • Cat-cow + thoracic rotations x 8/side

Optional upgrade: swap this for a GOWOD ACTIVATE routine — more targeted for climbers.

Gym B — Legs, Pull Power and Grip

Goal: heavy lower-body, weighted pulls for finger-independent pulling power, grip and core carries. ~60 min.

Warm-up (5-8 min)

  • 3 min easy bike
  • Bodyweight squats x 15
  • Walking lunges x 10/side
  • Glute bridges x 15
  • Dead bugs x 8/side

Optional upgrade: swap this for a GOWOD ACTIVATE routine.

Climb day guidelines

Intensity day (hard)

  • Thorough warm-up: 15 min on easy climbs
  • 4-6 hard attempts on 2-3 projects
  • Long rests (3-5 min) between attempts
  • Stop before form collapses (~90 min total)

Volume / technique day

  • Warm up, then 20-30 boulders at 2-3 grades below max
  • Focus on footwork, flagging, precise movement
  • Flash or 2nd-go rule — don't grind sessions
  • 60-75 min total

Mobility (powered by GOWOD)

Mobility is the biggest hidden bottleneck for climbers. Stiff hips kill high-steps, poor shoulder flexion limits reach, tight ankles make precision foot placement unstable. GOWOD tests your mobility and gives you targeted routines for your weak spots — exactly where climbers' gains hide.

When to do what

Before climbing

GOWOD ACTIVATE (5-10 min) wakes up shoulders, hips, and wrists before you hit the wall. Replaces your usual easy-grade warm-up with something much more targeted.

After climbing

GOWOD RECOVER (10 min) speeds recovery, reduces next-day stiffness. Do this before you leave the gym or within 30 min of getting home.

Rest days

GOWOD DAILY FLOW (10-15 min) is the long-term game. Consistency beats duration. 3-5x a week and your mobility score climbs month over month.

Focus areas for climbers

Upper body

  • Shoulder flexion and external rotation for overhangs, long reaches, mantles
  • Thoracic spine rotation for twisting moves, drop-knees
  • Wrist extension and flexion prevents climber's elbow downstream

Lower body

  • Hip flexion and external rotation the #1 gain for bouldering: high-steps, heel hooks, frog positions
  • Ankle dorsiflexion for precision footwork, drop-knees
  • Adductor length for wide stem positions and bridging

Fat loss: the 80/20 playbook

You don't need to count every calorie. Nail these five habits and fat loss happens without crashing your climbing.

The 5 habits

  1. Protein first: 1.6-2.2 g per kg bodyweight, daily.
  2. ~500 kcal deficit: aim for ~0.5 kg/week loss.
  3. Carbs around climbing: most carbs in the 2-3 hours before/after climbs.
  4. Hydrate and sleep 7-8h: non-negotiable.
  5. Alcohol max 2-3 units/week: torpedoes sleep and recovery.

Climb-day fuel

  • 2-3h before: 40-60g carbs + 20-30g protein
  • During: water + small handful of dried fruit if session is long
  • After: 30-40g protein within 1h
  • Rest day: drop 200-400 kcal vs training day, mostly from carbs

Cardio for fat loss (without killing climbing)

Do this

  • 2-4 times per week, 30-45 min zone-2 (brisk walk, easy bike, slow jog)
  • 10,000 steps/day target
  • Optional 15-min HIIT finisher after Gym B

Avoid this

  • Heavy running the day before climbing — trashes legs
  • HIIT on climb days — cuts into recovery
  • Fasted hard sessions — lowers intensity and muscle retention

Core principles

1. Climbing is the goal — gym supports it.
Never sacrifice climbing recovery for a gym PR.
2. Train antagonists.
Climbers get hunched and tight. Push, rear-delt, external-rotation work keeps shoulders healthy.
3. Progressive overload — slowly.
Add weight or 1 rep only when current weight feels 7/10 hard.
4. Protect your fingers.
No hangboarding while building a base. Climbing twice a week IS finger training.
5. Sleep beats supplements.
Sleep 7-8h. Creatine (5g/day) is the one supplement worth it.
6. Track honestly.
Weigh yourself 3x per week, same time. Track the trend, not the day.

Red flags — stop and reassess

  • Finger joint pain that persists more than 48h — skip climbing for a week, no hangs.
  • Elbow pain on the inside (medial epicondyle) — climber's elbow; do wrist extensor work and reduce volume.
  • Shoulder pinching overhead — check if you're skipping face pulls / rear delt work.
  • Weight loss greater than 1 kg/week for more than 2 weeks — too steep a deficit, eat more.
  • Climbing grades dropping consistently — almost always under-recovery or under-eating.
