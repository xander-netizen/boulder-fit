Your program at a glance

A 2-gym + 2-to-3-climb week is the sweet spot for bouldering + fat loss. The gym work is built around antagonists (push, rear delts, core), lower body strength (mostly ignored by climbers), and finger/grip conditioning — exactly the gaps that climbing alone won't fill.

Weekly template

  • Mon — Gym A (Push & Core)
  • Tue — Climb (technique + hard attempts)
  • Wed — Rest or 30 min zone-2 walk
  • Thu — Gym B (Legs & Pull)
  • Fri — Rest
  • Sat — Climb (volume / projects)
  • Sun — Optional climb (easy) or rest

Why this works for you

  • Climbing handles your pulling strength — gym fills push/leg gaps.
  • 48 hours between climb and gym = better recovery for fingers and forearms.
  • Core + anti-rotation carries over directly to harder boulders.
  • 2 strength days keeps fat-loss deficit tolerable without wrecking climbs.

Alternative splits

If you only climb twice a week

Mon Gym A · Tue Climb · Wed Rest · Thu Gym B · Fri Climb · Sat Zone-2 cardio (30–45 min) · Sun rest. You can optionally add a 3rd short gym day (20 min conditioning + core) on Saturday.

If you climb three times a week

Mon Gym A · Tue Climb · Wed Rest · Thu Climb · Fri Gym B · Sat Climb · Sun rest. Keep intensity high only 1–2 climb days per week; the third should be volume/technique.

Deload every 4th week

Every fourth week, drop gym volume by ~40% (fewer sets, not lighter weight) and climb at 70% intensity. You'll come back stronger and injury-free.

Gym A — Push, Antagonists & Core

Goal: balance all the pulling you do climbing. Strong push, rear-delt, and core make you injury-proof and shoulder-stable on hard moves. ~60 min.

Warm-up (5–8 min)

  • 3 min easy row or bike
  • Band pull-aparts × 20
  • Scap push-ups × 10
  • Wall slides × 10
  • Cat-cow + thoracic rotations × 8/side

Gym B — Legs, Pull Power & Grip

Goal: heavy lower-body (climbers neglect this and miss out on explosive heel hooks + metabolism), weighted pulls for finger-independent pulling power, grip/core carries. ~60 min.

Warm-up (5–8 min)

  • 3 min easy bike
  • Bodyweight squats × 15
  • Walking lunges × 10/side
  • Glute bridges × 15
  • Dead bugs × 8/side

Climb day guidelines

Intensity day (hard)

  • Thorough warm-up: 15 min on easy climbs
  • 4–6 hard attempts on 2–3 projects
  • Long rests (3–5 min) between attempts
  • Stop before form collapses (~90 min total)

Volume / technique day

  • Warm up, then 20–30 boulders at 2–3 grades below max
  • Focus on footwork, flagging, precise movement
  • Flash or 2nd-go rule — don't grind sessions
  • 60–75 min total

Fat loss: the 80/20 playbook

You don't need to count every calorie. Nail these five habits and fat loss happens without crashing your climbing.

The 5 habits

  1. Protein first: 1.6–2.2 g per kg bodyweight, daily. Preserves muscle in a deficit and helps you feel full.
  2. ~500 kcal deficit: aim for ~0.5 kg/week loss. Faster kills climbing performance.
  3. Carbs around climbing: eat most carbs in the 2–3 hours before/after climbs. Low-carb on rest days if you want.
  4. Hydrate + sleep 7–8h: under-slept lifters lose more muscle and perform worse. Non-negotiable.
  5. Alcohol ≤ 2–3 units/week: it torpedoes sleep, recovery, and body composition.

Climb-day fuel

  • 2–3h before: 40–60g carbs + 20–30g protein (oats + yogurt, rice + chicken, etc.)
  • During: water + small handful of dried fruit if session is long
  • After: 30–40g protein within 1h (shake, eggs, cottage cheese)
  • Rest day: drop 200–400 kcal vs training day, mostly from carbs

Cardio for fat loss (without killing climbing)

Do this

  • 2–4× per week, 30–45 min zone-2 (brisk walk, easy bike, slow jog) — on rest days or after gym
  • 10,000 steps/day target — low-hanging fruit
  • One optional 15-min HIIT finisher after Gym B (bike intervals)

Avoid this

  • Heavy running the day before climbing — trashes legs
  • HIIT on climb days — cuts into recovery
  • Fasted hard sessions — lowers intensity and muscle retention

Core principles

1. Climbing is the goal — gym supports it.
Never sacrifice climbing recovery for a gym PR. If forearms are cooked, skip the gym pull work that day and do legs only.
2. Train antagonists.
Climbers get hunched, internally rotated, and tight. Push, rear-delt, and external-rotation work keep your shoulders healthy for years.
3. Progressive overload — slowly.
Add weight or 1 rep only when the current weight feels "7/10 hard". Chasing PRs in a deficit = injury.
4. Protect your fingers.
No hangboarding while you're still building a base. Climbing twice a week IS finger training right now.
5. Sleep > supplements.
Sleep 7–8h. Creatine (5g/day) is the one supplement worth it. Protein powder if you can't hit protein otherwise.
6. Track honestly.
Weigh yourself 3× per week, same time, same conditions. Track the trend, not the day.

Exercise reference

Barbell bench press

Lie flat, retract shoulder blades, bar touches mid-sternum, drive up. Elbows at ~45° (not flared). Watch tutorial ▶

Overhead press

Standing, bar at collarbone. Brace hard, press overhead, bar path slightly back as head comes through. Watch tutorial ▶

Weighted dip

Chest slightly forward for chest emphasis; more upright for triceps. Lower until shoulders level with elbows. Watch tutorial ▶

Face pull

Cable + rope at face height. Pull to forehead with external rotation — thumbs point back at the end. Watch tutorial ▶

Back squat

Bar on upper traps, feet shoulder-width, knees track over toes. Break parallel if mobility allows. Watch tutorial ▶

Romanian deadlift (RDL)

Hinge at hips, slight knee bend, bar close to legs, back flat. Feel stretch in hamstrings at bottom. Watch tutorial ▶

Bulgarian split squat

Rear foot on bench, front foot far enough forward that knee tracks over midfoot. Drive through heel. Watch tutorial ▶

Weighted pull-up

Belt with plate or dumbbell between feet. Full hang to chin over bar. Add weight before reps. Watch tutorial ▶

Pallof press

Cable at chest height, side-on. Press straight out and resist rotation. Key anti-rotation core drill. Watch tutorial ▶

Farmer carry

Heavy DB or trap bar. Walk tall, shoulders packed, 30–40m. Builds grip, core, and traps. Watch tutorial ▶

Red flags — stop and reassess

  • Finger joint pain that persists more than 48h — skip climbing for a week, no hangs.
  • Elbow pain on the inside (medial epicondyle) — common climber's elbow; do wrist extensor work and reduce volume.
  • Shoulder pinching overhead — check if you're skipping face pulls / rear delt work.
  • Weight loss > 1 kg/week for more than 2 weeks — you're in too steep a deficit, eat more.
  • Climbing grades dropping consistently — almost always under-recovery or under-eating.
